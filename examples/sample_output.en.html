About the html-memo Skill

html-memo is a custom skill that runs inside Claude Code. With a single command, it saves your daily work logs and study notes as elegant HTML files styled in "serif typography × dark mode". Its goal is to elevate information — too often left to rot as plain Markdown — into documents you actually want to revisit.

A note is not at its peak the moment it is written; its value is reborn when it is read again. That is why visual beauty sustains the continuity of thought.

Design Specification

The background uses #111418 — softer than pure black — layered with a cool radial gradient and a fine noise texture. It stays gentle on the eyes during long reading sessions, while the cool-toned accent #5BB3C9 — a cyan associated with calm and focus — glows quietly across the page.

Typography

The body is set in a serif typeface that lends text dignity and readability. Section numbers and quotation marks pair in Cormorant Garamond, giving the page rhythm through its Latin character. Font sizes are kept restrained, preserving quiet, refined whitespace.

Usage

Type /html-memo in the Claude Code prompt, followed by what you want to note. The skill automatically generates an HTML file and saves it to the memos/ directory. 

/html-memo Summarize today's development and tomorrow's tasks

The generated file shines in situations like these:

  • When you want to keep daily development logs as records worth rereading
  • When you want study notes to accumulate as "documents", not scribbles
  • When you want research and design decisions formatted to share with a team

Multilingual Support

The skill automatically detects the language you speak to it in. Prompt it in Japanese and you get a Japanese memo; prompt it in English and you get an English memo — both in the same beautiful layout.

A tool for the writer, yet one that designs the reader's experience too. That is what html-memo strives to be.
